Victim's advocate

Victim's advocate – support and security after violence and abuse

If you have been subjected to violence or sexual abuse, you are usually entitled to a victim's advocate who accompanies you throughout the entire case – at no cost to you. The state covers the legal fees.

What is a victim's advocate?

A lawyer on your side – free of charge

The victim's advocate scheme (bistandsadvokatordningen) gives you, as the aggrieved party (fornærmede), the right to have your own lawyer at your side in the case, without paying for it. The scheme applies in particular to certain sexual offences and serious acts of violence. The victim's advocate looks after your interests from the first period after the incident all the way until the case is concluded.

Advokatfirmaet Torgersen AS has specialist expertise in personal injury compensation, criminal injuries compensation and social security law, and is a member of the Norwegian Bar Association (Advokatforeningen). Lawyer Anette Torgersen has long experience as a victim's advocate and knows the process well – from the first conversation until the compensation claim is decided.

What do we help with?

How the victim's advocate assists you

  • Application to the court. We write the application for the appointment of a victim's advocate for you.
  • Reporting to the police. We can help you report the matter to the police if this has not already been done.
  • Support during questioning. We are with you during police questioning and follow you closely throughout the entire process.
  • Appeal if the case is dropped. We can appeal the case if the police drop it.
  • Compensation claim. We bring the compensation claim on your behalf.
  • Your interests. We look after you during the investigation and any trial.
