Compensation

Compensation after a traffic accident

Were you hit while a driver, cyclist or pedestrian? Or did you drive into someone or off the road yourself? These are typical traffic accidents covered by the scheme, together with injuries caused by, among other things, a motorcycle or bus. Regardless of whether anyone can be blamed for the accident, you may be entitled to compensation if you suffered a personal injury.

In the event of a personal injury after a traffic accident, you may be entitled to, among other things, permanent-injury compensation (menerstatning), coverage of loss of income, loss of ability to perform work in the home, expenses and compensation for non-economic loss.

How the scheme works

Statutory insurance for vehicles

In Norway, all vehicles are required to be insured. The purpose is to protect anyone injured in traffic, while at the same time largely protecting the driver against personal liability for the compensation claim. The traffic accident is reported to the liable insurance company.

What if the vehicle that caused the accident was uninsured or unknown? Then you have the same rights as if there were a liable insurance company to deal with. In such cases, the matter is handled by the Norwegian Motor Insurers' Bureau (Trafikkforsikringsforeningen, TFF).

Good advice

Get information early

We recommend that you contact a personal injury lawyer already in the initial stage after the accident, so that you obtain the information you need and do not risk losing claims. What many people do not know is that the insurance company must, as a general rule, cover necessary and reasonable legal costs from this stage onwards.

In addition to reporting the matter to the liable insurance company, it may be necessary to notify several bodies – for example, if you are covered under private or collective schemes. We go through your entire case together, so that no opportunities are overlooked.

  • Permanent-injury compensation (menerstatning). Compensation for permanent medical disability following the injury.
  • Loss of income. Coverage of lost work income, both accrued and future.
  • Loss of ability to work in the home. Compensation for a reduced ability to perform work in the home.
  • Expenses and non-economic loss. Coverage of additional expenses and any compensation for non-economic loss.
Legal costs

Are the costs of legal assistance covered?

As a general rule, the liable insurance company must cover necessary and reasonable legal costs in the traffic injury case.
