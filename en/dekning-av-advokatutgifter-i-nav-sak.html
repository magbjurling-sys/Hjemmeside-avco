NAV / benefits

Coverage of legal costs in a NAV case

In many NAV cases the legal costs can be covered in full or in part through schemes such as free legal advice (fritt rettsråd) and free legal representation (fri sakførsel). Here is an overview of what applies at the various stages.

Application

Work on the application for a NAV benefit

Legal costs for work on the application itself are as a rule not covered. There are, however, exceptions, including where you can document a personal injury and meet the conditions for free legal aid (fri rettshjelp).

Appeal

Work on the appeal to the NAV Appeals Unit

After a refusal from NAV Administration (NAV Forvaltning), you may be entitled to free legal advice (fritt rettsråd) for the appeal, provided you meet the conditions. The patient's contribution depends on your financial situation and corresponds to one times the public legal-fee rate.

Even without free legal advice, the legal costs may be covered if NAV reverses the decision in your favour.

Further appeal

Work on the appeal to the National Insurance Court

When appealing to the National Insurance Court (Trygderetten), you may be entitled to free legal representation (fri sakførsel), provided you meet the conditions. The patient's contribution is 25% of the total legal costs, but at most 8 times the public rate.

As with the appeal, the costs may also be covered if the National Insurance Court reverses the decision in your favour.
