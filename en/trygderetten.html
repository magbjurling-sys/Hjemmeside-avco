Appeal

Appeal to the National Insurance Court

The National Insurance Court (Trygderetten) is an independent body that handles appeals against decisions on social security and pension matters. If both NAV Administration (NAV Forvaltning) and the NAV Appeals Unit (NAV Klageinstans) have refused your case, the case can be appealed further to the National Insurance Court.

Proceedings before the National Insurance Court are written, so you do not need to appear in court. When the case has been concluded, the National Insurance Court issues a ruling in the case.

Legal costs

Are the legal costs covered?

Are the legal costs covered when appealing to the National Insurance Court?

In many cases the legal costs of the work on the appeal are covered. We clarify what applies to your case when you get in touch. Read more on the page about coverage of legal costs in a NAV case.

Yes. Lawyer Anette Torgersen specialises in social security law and has extensive experience appealing various types of refusal decisions to the National Insurance Court.
