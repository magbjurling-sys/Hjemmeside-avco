Family law

Child custody and residence

When parents separate, it must be decided where the child is going to live and how everyday life is to be organised. The best interests of the child are always decisive – and we help you find a safe solution.

Permanent residence

Where should the child live after a relationship breakdown?

Permanent residence (fast bosted) concerns where the child has its main base and spends most of its time. The parents can themselves agree that the child is to have permanent residence with one of them, or that there is to be shared permanent residence (delt fast bosted).

In the case of relationship breakdown, separation and parental disputes where you have children together under the age of 16, it is mandatory to attend mediation at the Family Counselling Office (familievernkontoret). If the parents do not reach agreement, they must as a general rule have attended mediation before the case can potentially be brought before the courts.

The best interests of the child

What is given weight in the assessment?

When it is to be decided where the child is to live, the best interests of the child are the decisive consideration. There is no standard solution that fits all families – the assessment must be made specifically. Key factors include:

  • The child's age
  • The need for stability and continuity
  • The child's attachment to each of the parents
  • The care situation with each parent
  • The parents' ability to cooperate

For younger children, the consideration of continuity and a secure base for care will often carry particular weight. Children also have the right to be heard in matters concerning their own situation, and the child's opinion is to be given weight according to age and maturity.

How we help

Support in demanding cases

Cases concerning child custody and permanent residence can be difficult and marked by a high level of conflict between the parents, particularly where there is disagreement about what is best for the child. Advokatfirmaet Torgersen has experience with cases concerning permanent residence and shared permanent residence, and assists, among other things, in negotiations, mediation and legal proceedings.

What is the difference between permanent residence and contact?

Permanent residence concerns where the child has its main base and spends most of its time. Contact concerns how the child is to spend time with the parent it does not live with permanently.

What is shared permanent residence?

Shared permanent residence means that the child has permanent residence with both parents. The parents can themselves agree on such an arrangement. Whether it is in the best interests of the child must be assessed specifically in each individual case.

Do we have to go to court to decide residence?

No. Parents can themselves agree on permanent residence or shared permanent residence. If you do not reach agreement, you must as a general rule have attended mediation before the case can potentially be brought before the courts.
