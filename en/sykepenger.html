NAV / benefits

Sickness benefit

If you are injured or fall ill, you may be entitled to sickness benefit (sykepenger) to cover the income you lose. If you have been refused or disagree with the calculation, we will help you with the appeal.

The right to sickness benefit

What are you entitled to?

Sickness benefit is intended to compensate for lost income when you are unable to work because of illness or injury. As an employee, you are in principle entitled to full income compensation for up to one year, for income up to 6 G (the National Insurance basic amount, G). Your employer covers the first 16 days, after which NAV takes over.

If you earn more than 6 G per year, it is worth checking whether you are also covered by other schemes. If the absence is due to a traffic accident, a workplace accident or an act of violence, you may be entitled to further compensation through separate insurance schemes.

Refusal

Have you been refused sickness benefit by NAV?

Unfortunately, a number of incorrect decisions are made at NAV. If you disagree with the decision, it is important that you make use of your right to appeal, and that you do so within the appeal deadline. A case may concern sickness benefit being refused altogether, or the calculation of the benefit itself being incorrect.

Are the legal costs covered when appealing a refusal of sickness benefit?

In many cases the legal costs of the work on the appeal are covered. We review your situation and clarify what applies to your particular case.

What is the appeal deadline?

There is a specific deadline for appealing the decision from NAV. It is important that the deadline is met. Get in touch as soon as possible and we will help you get started.
