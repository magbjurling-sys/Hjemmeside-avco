Privacy policy

Here we explain how Advokatfirmaet Torgersen processes personal data, what rights you have, and how we use cookies.

Data controller

Advokatfirmaet Torgersen AS (org.nr. 819 854 602), Olav Brunborgs vei 6, 1396 Billingstad, is the data controller for the personal data that is processed. Questions about privacy can be directed to post@advokattorgersen.no or by telephone on 98 67 29 29.

What data we process

We process the information you provide to us yourself when you get in touch – for example your name, email address, telephone number and details about your case. When you are a client, we also process the information necessary to carry out the engagement, including documentation in the case.

Purpose and legal basis

  • Enquiries. We use your contact details to respond to you and assess whether we can assist. The basis is your enquiry and our legitimate interest.
  • Engagements. When we take on an engagement, we process information in order to fulfil the agreement and our duties as a lawyer.
  • Statutory obligations. As a law firm, we are subject to, among other things, accounting and anti-money-laundering regulations.

Duty of confidentiality

Lawyers are subject to a strict statutory duty of confidentiality. Information about you and your case is treated confidentially and is not shared with others without your consent, unless we are legally obliged to do so.

Storage and deletion

We store information for as long as it is necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, and in accordance with lawyers' duty to archive and other regulations. Enquiries that do not lead to an engagement are deleted when they are no longer relevant.

Cookies

The website does not, in principle, use cookies for analytics or marketing. The map on the contact page is loaded from Google Maps, and your browser may then receive cookies from Google. You can control and delete cookies in your browser settings.

Your rights

You have the right to access, rectify and delete your own personal data, and to request restriction of the processing. You can also lodge a complaint with the Norwegian Data Protection Authority (Datatilsynet) if you believe the processing is in breach of data protection regulations. Please get in touch with us first, and we will help you.
