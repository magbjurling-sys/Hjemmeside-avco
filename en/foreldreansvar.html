Family law

Parental responsibility

Parental responsibility is about the right and the duty to make important decisions on behalf of the child. When parents disagree about how this responsibility should be exercised, conflicts can arise – we help you find a good way forward.

What does it involve?

What does parental responsibility involve?

Parental responsibility covers the important decisions in the child's life, including questions about health, schooling and other personal matters. This is a legal responsibility that applies regardless of where the child lives permanently.

When parents have joint parental responsibility, they must as a general rule agree on the major decisions concerning the child. The parent the child lives with permanently can nevertheless make decisions about the day-to-day care and everyday life. It is not always equally clear where the line lies between decisions that require agreement and decisions that can be made alone – and it is precisely this that can give rise to conflict.

As the child grows older, the child's own views are given greater weight. Children have the right to be heard in matters that concern them, and their opinion is to form part of the basis for the decisions.

In the event of disagreement

What do you do if you disagree?

Many families resolve disagreements through good communication and cooperation. When this is not possible – particularly where cooperation between the parents is difficult, or where there is concern about the child's care situation – there may be a need for legal clarification.

Advokatfirmaet Torgersen has experience with cases concerning parental responsibility and assists, among other things, with:

  • Assessment of rights and obligations
  • Negotiations between the parties
  • Drafting of agreements
  • Court proceedings if necessary
Questions and answers

Frequently asked questions

What kinds of decisions require joint parental responsibility?

Joint parental responsibility means that the parents must, as a general rule, agree on the important decisions in the child's life, for example key questions about health and schooling. The day-to-day care and everyday decisions can be made by the parent the child lives with permanently.

What does parental responsibility mean for where the child lives?

Parental responsibility is a legal responsibility that applies regardless of where the child has its permanent base. The question of where the child is to live is dealt with separately under permanent residence and child custody.

Does the child get to take part in the decision?

Children have the right to be heard in matters concerning them, and the child's opinion is to be given weight according to age and maturity. The older the child is, the greater the significance of the child's own views.
