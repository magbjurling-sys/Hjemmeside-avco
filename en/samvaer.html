Contact

How it is decided

How is contact arranged after a relationship breakdown?

When the child has permanent residence with one of the parents, it must also be clarified how the contact with the other parent is to be arranged. The purpose of contact is to allow the child to maintain a secure and good relationship with both parents, also after a relationship breakdown.

The parents are largely free to agree on the extent of the contact and how it is to take place. Many choose to enter into written agreements that regulate weekdays, holidays, special occasions and other practical matters. A clear agreement makes everyday life more predictable – both for the child and for the parents.

The best interests of the child

The child at the centre

In the assessment of contact, the best interests of the child are always the decisive consideration. What is the best arrangement must be assessed specifically on the basis of the child's needs and situation. Children have the right to be heard in matters concerning contact, and the child's opinion is to be given weight according to age and maturity.

Advokatfirmaet Torgersen has experience with contact cases and assists, among other things, with:

  • Assessment of contact arrangements
  • Negotiations between parents
  • Court proceedings when needed
Questions and answers

Frequently asked questions

Can we agree on the contact ourselves?

Yes. The parents are largely free to agree on the extent of the contact and how it is to take place. Many choose a written agreement that regulates weekdays, holidays, special occasions and other practical matters.

What is given weight when contact is determined?

The best interests of the child are always the decisive consideration. The specific arrangement is assessed on the basis of the child's needs and situation, and the child has the right to be heard according to age and maturity.

What happens if we cannot agree on contact?

If you do not reach agreement, a lawyer can assist with negotiations, and the case can, if necessary, be brought before the courts. Please feel free to get in touch for a no-obligation conversation about your situation.
