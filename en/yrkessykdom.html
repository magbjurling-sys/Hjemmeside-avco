Compensation

Compensation after an occupational disease

You may be entitled to occupational injury compensation regardless of fault. Typical conditions that may give rise to a compensation claim include asthma and other lung diseases, poisoning injuries, injuries following chemical exposure, hearing loss and allergic reactions. Certain occupational groups are particularly exposed, for example bakers, hairdressers and industrial workers.

The assessment of when the illness is to be regarded as established, and which insurance company is to handle the matter, can be complicated. As a general rule, it is the insurance company of the employer you had when you first consulted a doctor about the illness that is liable.

In the event of an occupational disease, you may be entitled to, among other things, permanent-injury compensation (menerstatning), coverage of loss of income, loss of ability to perform work in the home, and expenses. If you are employed by the state or a municipality, you may in addition have special rights under the Basic Collective Agreement (Hovedtariffavtalen). The compensation is calculated under several sets of rules, and you choose the one that gives you the best result.

How the scheme works

Statutory occupational injury insurance

Employers are required by law to take out occupational injury insurance for their employees. If the employer has not taken out insurance, you still have the same rights. In such cases, the matter is handled by the Occupational Injury Insurance Association (Yrkesskadeforsikringsforeningen, YFF).

Important to know

Reporting an occupational disease

The employer must report the occupational disease to both NAV and the insurance company. Experience unfortunately shows that many employers neglect this duty, which may be to your detriment. You can report the matter yourself or get help from a lawyer. What many people do not know is that the insurance company must, as a general rule, cover necessary and reasonable legal costs from the very initial stage.

In addition to reporting to NAV and the insurance company, there may be a need to notify several bodies – for example, in the case of private or collective schemes.

Legal costs

Are the costs of legal assistance covered?

In the event of an occupational disease, you are, as a general rule, entitled to have the liable insurance company cover necessary and reasonable legal costs in the matter.
