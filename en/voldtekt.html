Victim's advocate

Compensation and assistance after rape

If you have been subjected to rape or attempted rape, you are as a rule entitled to have a victim's advocate appointed. The lawyer accompanies you from the first period after the incident until the case is concluded – entirely at no cost to you. The state pays the legal fees.

Compensation

Compensation claim after rape or attempted rape

If you have been subjected to rape or attempted rape, you may, among other things, be entitled to permanent injury compensation (menerstatning), coverage of loss of income, loss of homemaking capacity, expenses and compensation for non-economic loss (oppreisning).

What does the law say?

What is rape?

Put simply, it is about being forced into sexual activity against your will. This can, for example, be forced intercourse, or being forced into sexual acts with the perpetrator or others.

The Penal Code (straffeloven) describes rape in more detail. It is considered rape, among other situations, if a person:

  • Use of violence or threatening behaviour. Someone obtains sexual activity through violence or threats.
  • Unable to resist. Someone engages in sexual activity with a person who is unable to resist the act.
  • Coercion into activity with others. Someone, through violence or threats, makes a person engage in sexual activity with another.
  • Coercion into acts with oneself. Someone, through violence or threats, makes a person perform acts corresponding to sexual activity with themselves.
  • Children under 14. Sexual activity with a child under 14 is considered rape, regardless of any consent.

Even if you have previously flirted, kissed or had intercourse, it is rape if the sexual activity this time occurred through violence, coercion or while you were unable to resist it.

A no is a no. It can be expressed both with words and body language. If this is not respected, it is abuse. If you are asleep or unconscious, you cannot say a clear no – such cases too can constitute rape.

Rape can be difficult to talk about. Often it is someone you already know who has done this to you. Sometimes it has been a while since it happened, and only now have you become strong enough to address it. In any case, you are welcome to get in touch.
