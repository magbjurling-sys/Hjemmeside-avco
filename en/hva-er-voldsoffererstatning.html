Criminal injuries compensation

What is criminal injuries compensation?

Criminal injuries compensation is, in short, compensation for you who have suffered a personal injury as a result of violence. If you have been subjected to a criminal act, you may be entitled to have both financial losses and non-economic damages (oppreisning) covered.

The compensation claim

What can you claim compensation for after violence?

When you have suffered an injury as a result of violence, you may be entitled to compensation for several different items. Which claims are relevant will depend on your specific situation, but the most common are:

  • Compensation for past and future loss of income
  • Compensation for loss of the ability to perform work in the home
  • Coverage of expenses related to the injury
  • Permanent disability compensation (menerstatning) in the case of lasting medical injury
  • Non-economic damages (oppreisning) for the violation you have been subjected to
KFV

The Norwegian Criminal Injuries Compensation Authority (KFV)

The compensation claim is normally handled by the Norwegian Criminal Injuries Compensation Authority (Kontoret for voldsoffererstatning, KFV). An important advantage of this scheme is that you, as the injured party, do not have to deal directly with the perpetrator in order to have the compensation paid out. The KFV processes your application after the case has been through the police and court system.

After the judgment

Judgment in the criminal case

Even if the perpetrator has been ordered to pay compensation, it sometimes happens that the amount is not paid. In such cases, you can apply to have the compensation paid out through the KFV. It is also worth being aware that you may be entitled to further compensation than that awarded in the judgment itself.

Dismissal

When the criminal case has been dismissed

Many people believe that the right to compensation lapses if the case has been dismissed by the police. That is not necessarily true. The standard of proof is different in a compensation case than in a criminal case, and you may therefore be entitled to criminal injuries compensation even if the criminal case has been dropped. If you are in doubt, it is worth having the case assessed.

Questions and answers

Frequently asked questions

Are legal costs covered in dealings with the KFV?

Whether your legal costs are covered depends, among other things, on the scope and complexity of the case. You can read more on the page about coverage of legal costs.

Can I receive compensation even if the case has been dismissed?

Yes, you can. Because the standard of proof is lower in a compensation case than in a criminal case, you may be entitled to criminal injuries compensation even if the police have dismissed the criminal case.
