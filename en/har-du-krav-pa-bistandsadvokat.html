Victim's advocate

Are you entitled to a victim's advocate?

If you have been subjected to violence, you can get in touch for an assessment of whether you can have a victim's advocate appointed. Some incidents of violence give an automatic right, while for others it must be assessed how significant your injury is and whether other special circumstances exist. It is the court that decides.

Cost

What does it cost you?

If a victim's advocate is appointed in the case, the state pays the legal fees for you. You also pay no deductible. You therefore have no legal costs in the case. Lawyer Anette Torgersen can write the application for you.

General rule

When are you, as a rule, entitled to a victim's advocate?

Here are some examples of incidents of violence that, as a general rule, entitle you to a victim's advocate. Since several situations may give the right, we recommend that you get in touch, so that lawyer Anette Torgersen can help you find out whether your particular situation gives the right to a victim's advocate.

Violence in close relationships

Violence, threats or coercion from someone close to you

If you have been subjected to violence, threats or coercion from someone close to you, you are in principle entitled to a victim's advocate. It is enough that the act has occurred once if it is serious. If the act has occurred several times, less serious cases are also covered.

The law specifies the following as someone close to you:

  • Spouse or cohabitant. Your former or current spouse or cohabitant.
  • Stepchildren and similar. A relative in the direct descending line of your former or current spouse or cohabitant, for example a stepchild.
  • Parents and grandparents. Your relative in the direct ascending line, for example a parent.
  • Someone in your home. A person who belongs to your household.
  • Someone in your care. A person for whom you have caregiving responsibility.
Further grounds

Rape and significant injury

  • Rape. If you have been subjected to rape or attempted rape, you are as a rule entitled to a victim's advocate.
  • Significant injury. If you have suffered a significant injury as a result of the violence, you are as a rule entitled to a victim's advocate.
