Criminal injuries compensation

Coverage of legal costs in a criminal injuries case

Many people wonder what it costs to get help from a lawyer after an act of violence. In several cases, your legal costs can be covered through public schemes. Here you will find an overview of the most important options.

Victim's advocate

Coverage through a victim's advocate

If the act of violence qualifies for the right to a victim's advocate (bistandsadvokat), the legal costs are covered by the state. This applies from the incident until the criminal case is concluded, and it is free of charge for you who have been subjected to the violence. You can read more on our page about the victim's advocate.

Free legal aid

Free legal aid

For acts of violence that do not give a right to a victim's advocate, you can in some cases apply for free legal aid (fri rettshjelp). The scheme provides a limited number of hours of legal assistance, for example in connection with an insurance claim. Whether you have to pay an excess depends on your individual circumstances.

KFV

Coverage from the Norwegian Criminal Injuries Compensation Authority

If the scope or complexity of the case warrants it, the Norwegian Criminal Injuries Compensation Authority (Kontoret for voldsoffererstatning, KFV) can cover legal costs related to the handling of the compensation claim. In each individual case, we assess which schemes may be relevant for you.
