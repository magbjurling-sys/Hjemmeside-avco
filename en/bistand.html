Victim's advocate

What is the victim's advocate scheme?

The victim's advocate scheme (bistandsadvokatordningen) means that you have the right to your own lawyer who assists you in the case – at no cost to you. The scheme applies in particular to certain sexual offences and serious acts of violence.

How it works

A lawyer at your side – paid for by the state

As the aggrieved party (fornærmede) in a criminal case concerning violence or abuse, you are usually entitled to a victim's advocate. The lawyer is there for you alone and looks after your interests throughout the entire process. The state covers the legal fees, and you pay no deductible.

What do we help with?

What can the victim's advocate help you with?

  • Application to the district court. We help you apply for the appointment of a victim's advocate.
  • Reporting to the police. We can assist with reporting the matter to the police if this has not already been done.
  • Support during questioning. We are with you during police questioning and follow you closely throughout the entire case.
  • Appeal if the case is dropped. We can appeal if the police drop the case.
  • Compensation claim. We bring the compensation claim on your behalf.
  • Your interests. We look after you during the investigation and any trial.
