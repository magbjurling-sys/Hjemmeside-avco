Permanent-injury compensation

The right to permanent-injury compensation

When can you apply?

If you have had an occupational injury or occupational illness approved by NAV, you can apply for permanent-injury compensation. To be entitled to permanent-injury compensation, the general rule is that a medical invalidity of at least 15% is required.

What is permanent-injury compensation?

Compensation for loss of quality of life

Permanent-injury compensation is compensation for loss of quality of life as a result of a permanent injury. The size of the compensation depends, among other things, on how serious the permanent injury is.

The case should be assessed, and the degree of invalidity determined, by a person with medical expertise in the relevant field for your injury. The choice of specialist has a major impact on the outcome, because the assessments often rest on discretion. The rules give you the opportunity to take part in deciding which specialist is to assess your case, and legal assistance can therefore be valuable.
