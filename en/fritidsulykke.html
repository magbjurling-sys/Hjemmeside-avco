Compensation

Compensation after a leisure-time accident

How much you can claim in compensation, and which bodies you have to deal with, depends among other things on the type of accident you have been involved in and the injuries you have sustained. There are several different bases for compensation, and it is important to map out all the options.

Basis

Liability cover and direct claims

For certain accidents there is insurance cover based on strict liability, which means that no one needs to be at fault for the accident. The compensation claim is then sent to the liable insurance company – for example, if you were involved in a traffic accident during your leisure time.

If the accident does not fall under a scheme based on strict liability, there are other types of liability cover. If a person caused the accident, it can be checked whether they have liability insurance, which is often part of the home contents insurance. If the person does not have such insurance, it may be considered whether to direct the compensation claim against them directly. To be able to claim compensation on this basis, it is, as a starting point, required that the person who caused the accident acted negligently.

Further options

Private and collective insurance schemes

Even if you have a liable party to direct the claim against, you may in addition be entitled to compensation under private or collective insurance schemes. The accident should therefore also be reported under these schemes.

For certain accidents there is no liability cover – for example, in the case of falls during leisure time where no one can be held responsible. Then it is the private and collective schemes from which you may possibly claim compensation. Accident insurance is one such scheme. You may be covered under several accident policies at the same time, and may receive compensation for a permanent injury under each of them. It is therefore important to notify all of them, and to check which policies you have privately, through your employer and through trade unions.

If there is a liable party to bring a claim against, you may be entitled to, among other things, permanent-injury compensation (menerstatning), coverage of loss of income, loss of ability to perform work in the home, and expenses. What you can claim under an accident policy depends on the policy terms, but it is common to include, among other things, disability compensation and coverage of expenses.

Legal costs

Are the costs of legal assistance covered?

In many cases with strict liability cover, necessary and reasonable legal costs are covered. For assistance in connection with accident insurance, the costs may be covered through the legal aid scheme (fri rettshjelp) if you meet the conditions. If a dispute arises in the matter, you may be entitled to coverage of legal costs through the legal-expenses insurance (rettshjelpsdekning) in your home contents insurance. Whether you have to pay an excess under the legal aid scheme or the legal-expenses cover depends on your financial situation.
