Compensation

Compensation after a workplace accident

In the event of a personal injury after a workplace accident, you may be entitled to, among other things, permanent-injury compensation (menerstatning), coverage of loss of income, loss of ability to perform work in the home, and expenses. If you are employed by the state or a municipality, you may in addition have special rights under the Basic Collective Agreement (Hovedtariffavtalen, HTA). The compensation is calculated under several sets of rules, and you may choose the set of rules that gives you the best result.

How the scheme works

Statutory occupational injury insurance

In Norway, employers are required by law to take out occupational injury insurance for their employees. This gives you security if an accident occurs and you are injured at work. The workplace accident is reported to the liable insurance company.

If the employer has not taken out the statutory insurance, you should not be the one to suffer. You have the same rights as if the insurance had been taken out, and the matter is instead reported to the Occupational Injury Insurance Association (Yrkesskadeforsikringsforeningen, YFF).

Important to know

Reporting a workplace accident

The employer must report the workplace accident to both NAV and the occupational injury insurer, and further communication in the matter takes place with these bodies. Unfortunately, many employers neglect this duty, which may be to your detriment. It is therefore important that you make sure the accident is in fact reported.

You can report the accident yourself, or get help from a lawyer. We recommend that you contact a personal injury lawyer already in the initial stage, so that you obtain the necessary information and do not lose claims. You then decide for yourself whether you wish to handle the matter on your own or let us assist you. What many people do not know is that the insurance company must, as a general rule, cover necessary and reasonable legal costs from this stage onwards.

Further options

Additional coverage after a workplace accident

In addition to reporting to NAV and the occupational injury insurer, it may be necessary to notify several bodies – for example, if you are covered under private or collective schemes. We go through your entire case together, so that you obtain the information you need.

  • Permanent-injury compensation (menerstatning). Compensation for permanent medical disability.
  • Loss of income. Coverage of lost work income.
  • Loss of ability to work in the home. Compensation for a reduced ability to perform work in the home.
  • Expenses. Coverage of additional expenses arising from the injury.
Legal costs

Are the costs of legal assistance covered?

After a workplace accident, you are, as a general rule, entitled to have the liable insurance company or the Occupational Injury Insurance Association cover necessary and reasonable legal costs in the occupational injury case.
