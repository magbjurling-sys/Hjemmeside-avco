Compensation

Compensation after medical malpractice

If there is a failure in the provision of health care, you may be entitled to compensation when you have suffered a personal injury. Exceptionally, you may be entitled to compensation even where there is no failure – in such cases it is assessed whether the injury is particularly serious or unexpected.

In the event of a personal injury after medical malpractice, you may be entitled to, among other things, permanent-injury compensation (menerstatning), coverage of loss of income, loss of ability to perform work in the home, and expenses.

How the scheme works

The Norwegian System of Patient Injury Compensation (NPE)

Whether the malpractice occurred in the public or the private health service, it is covered by the scheme. In both cases, the application is sent to the Norwegian System of Patient Injury Compensation (Norsk pasientskadeerstatning, NPE), which handles the matter.

If you receive a rejection from the NPE, the decision can be appealed to the Patient Injury Compensation Board (Pasientskadenemnda, PSN). The National Office for Health Service Appeals (Helseklage) is the secretariat for the board and prepares the case.

Legal costs

Are the costs of legal assistance covered?

In patient injury cases where the NPE has accepted that there was a failure in the health care, the NPE must cover necessary and reasonable legal costs for the work on the calculation of compensation. In special cases, legal costs from the initial stage – while liability is being investigated – are also covered.
