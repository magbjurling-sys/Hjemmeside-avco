Victim's advocate

Compensation after violence causing significant injury

If you have been subjected to a criminal act that resulted in significant injury, you are as a rule entitled to have a victim's advocate appointed who accompanies you from start to finish in the case. The state pays the legal fees.

Compensation

Compensation claim after significant violent injury

If you have been subjected to violence, the items of compensation may, among other things, include permanent injury compensation (menerstatning), coverage of loss of income, loss of homemaking capacity, expenses and compensation for non-economic loss (oppreisning).

The assessment

What is a significant violent injury?

The court makes a discretionary overall assessment of whether your injury should be regarded as significant. In the assessment, the court considers, among other things:

  • Length of sick leave. How long you have been unable to work or function.
  • Visible injuries. Whether the incident has left visible or lasting marks.
  • Reduced mobility. Whether your mobility is impaired.
  • Psychological diagnosis. Whether you have received a psychological diagnosis as a result of the incident.
  • Severity of the injury. How serious the injury is overall.

It is important that all the key factors are included in the application to the court, so that your case is presented as fully as possible.
